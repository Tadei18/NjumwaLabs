## The problem

The client ran several retail outlets plus an online store, but inventory lived in spreadsheets updated by hand. Popular items sold out before anyone noticed, while slow movers tied up cash. Reordering was reactive and error-prone, and nobody had a clear, real-time picture of stock.

## The approach

We centralised stock data into a single PostgreSQL source of truth, then layered automation on top:

- Sales from the online store and point-of-sale synced automatically into the database.
- An automation engine (n8n) tracked stock levels against per-product thresholds.
- When stock dipped below the reorder point, the system drafted purchase orders and pinged the manager on WhatsApp for one-tap approval.
- A Power BI dashboard gave live visibility into stock, sell-through and cash tied up in inventory.

## The stack

PostgreSQL for the data layer, Node.js services for the sync jobs, n8n for the orchestration and approvals, the WhatsApp Business API for notifications, and Power BI for reporting.

## The result

Within two months, stockouts on key lines dropped by roughly **73%**, and the team got back about **12 hours a week** previously spent updating spreadsheets and chasing reorders. Just as importantly, leadership finally had a reliable, real-time view of inventory.
